Corporate Governance & Organizational Resilience
Governance in the Trenches
From compliance to the real world
Corporate governance is often taught through policy manuals and compliance checklists.
I learned it in the trenches—where decisions are made under pressure, resources are thin, politics are real and failure carries human consequences.
My professional journey has taken me through leadership roles in audit firms, non-governmental organizations and business ventures. Along the way, I have seen projects shut down overnight, partnerships collapse under internal conflict and institutions lose direction due to poor governance choices.
I have also witnessed the human cost of governance failure: jobs lost, trust eroded, reputations damaged and livelihoods destabilized.
It is from these lived experiences that Governance in the Trenches was born.
Why “Governance in the Trenches”?
I did not learn governance from textbooks alone.
I learned it in environments where:
* Projects were abruptly terminated
* Organizations folded due to resource constraints
* Partnerships disintegrated under leadership conflict
* Leaders faced ethical dilemmas with no easy answers
* Jobs were lost through decisions made far removed from those affected
These experiences revealed a simple truth: governance is rarely tested in ideal conditions. It is tested in uncertainty—when margins are thin, information is incomplete, and leaders must decide under pressure.
Governance in the Trenches speaks directly to leaders navigating these realities.
Our Governance Philosophy
At Anchorpoint Resilience Solutions, corporate governance is approached as a resilience system—designed to help organizations anticipate shocks, withstand disruption and recover with integrity.
Much like mountain climbing, organizational survival depends on:
* Clear roles and accountability
* Sound preparation and risk awareness
* Ethical leadership under pressure
* Disciplined decision-making
* The ability to adapt when conditions change
Poor governance on a mountain can cost lives.
Poor governance in an organization can cost people their futures.
Our focus is to help leaders build governance systems that are practical, ethical and resilient—not merely compliant.
